Syllabus

The purpose of this course is to give you hands-on experience creating websites with interactive maps combining different types of spatial data. The course introduces the technology behind the maps–geographic information systems, server software, data sources, and web development languages. The course will raise an awareness of these technologies’ capabilities and limitations, to build your capacity for further learning and decision-making in fields that produce or consume web-based maps.

By the end of this course, you should be able to create a website displaying interactive maps driven by spatial data, and apply your knowledge in academic and professional fields.

Topics

The technology of Web GIS that makes it all possible, such as web servers and web services

Using Web GIS software with ArcGIS Online, an industry standard in GIS

Building your own Web GIS with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

Prerequisites

While there are no prerequisite courses, you must be computer literate to take this course. You should be familiar with common tasks such as using a web browser; downloading, opening, zipping, renaming, copying, and organizing files and folders; installing software; editing documents and spreadsheets with a text editor or Excel; and navigating to files in Windows.

Experience with GIS and programming would be an advantage, but is not assumed.

Assignments

The ten assignments are based on lectures and exercises covered in class, and include tasks such as building maps in ArcGIS Online and creating web pages.

Quizzes

Four quizzes will be given based on lecture material, testing on terminology and concepts covered in class.

Final project

The final project will be an opportunity to employ the concepts taught in the course in a website. The website will offer maps and other media to present information to your audience. The application must meet the core requirements defined in the project guidelines, but you will choose the best technologies and methods to deliver a satisfactory product.

Mid-term and final exams

The two exams will pull material from lectures. They are meant to ensure that you leave the course not only proficient with web GIS software but conversant in web GIS concepts.

Late submissions

An assignment not submitted by the deadline will lose a letter grade, and 5 points (out of 100) for each additional 24-hour period that the assignment is delinquent. Assignments, exams, and projects not completed by grade submission deadlines (midterm and final) must be graded as a zero. Home computer and Internet troubles will not be acceptable excuses for late submissions. An extension may be granted with an excused absence from class.

Expectations outside of class

Assignments that cannot be completed during class will require you to finish the work on your own computer or in a campus computer lab, such as the Geography and Anthropology computer lab in Howe-Russell E220. Time spent on an assignment outside of class might be expected to mirror the time spent on the related topics in class, including the lectures and exercises.
